Why the blackboard?

Because that is where this physics actually happened.

Between 1940 and 1965, physics was argued into existence at the board — at Los Alamos and Bell Labs, at Shelter Island and Copenhagen. The blackboard was the era's native medium: slow enough to think with, public enough to be challenged. This archive teaches that physics in its own dialect — chalk lines, hand-drawn diagrams, and derivations you can follow stroke by stroke.

It came from the blockspace! Cyberphysics pairs this classical archive with its research on blockchain-based AI self-models — the physics of mind. The lessons here are the foundations that work stands on.
